About this gallery

A selection of my favourite photos that I've shot on a Fujifilm X100S — sometimes around Toronto, where I live, and often on travels elsewhere in Canada or around the world.

Hosted on Cloudinary, which negotiates the format (AVIF, WebP, or JPEG) based on what your browser accepts and tunes compression per image, so you get the smallest file your device can still render cleanly. Thumbnails load at 800px wide and the masonry grid restacks itself from four columns down to one as the viewport narrows.

Hovering over a thumbnail quietly fetches the full 2400px version in the background, and the lightbox preloads its neighbors so prev/next feels instant. The slideshow opens fullscreen, crossfades every five seconds with one photo always queued up, and asks Cloudinary for an image sized to your display's actual pixel dimensions — so 4K and ultra-wide monitors get something sharper than the standard lightbox source.
